I Run 4 AI Agents From One Telegram Chat. Here's What Each One Does.

Published: 2026-02-28 · 6 min read

Most people's AI setup is one ChatGPT window. That's not a workflow. That's a parlor trick.

The people getting real leverage are running specialized agents with focused roles and context, then routing tasks to the right one.

Why Specialization Beats One Generalist

A general-purpose AI can do many tasks, but it rarely excels at any without heavy supervision. Role-specific agents with focused instructions create higher consistency and lower correction overhead.

The Setup: Four Agents, One Interface

All four run inside Telegram — one group, multiple topics, clear responsibilities:

  • Enoch — The Ops Agent: routing, coordination, scheduling, and operational triage.
  • Ezra — The Writing Agent: blog posts, social content, newsletters, and drafts in brand voice.
  • Solomon — The Strategy Agent: review, alignment, and strategic gatekeeping before publication.
  • Bezzy — The Coder: site changes, scripts, automation, and implementation.

What This Looks Like in Practice

Content gets drafted, reviewed, approved, and deployed in sequence — often overnight. Meanwhile, operational asks (like reminders and follow-ups) get routed in parallel without interrupting client work.

The point isn't complexity. It's architecture.

Where People Go Wrong

  • One agent for everything — leads to role confusion and mixed-quality output.
  • No persistent context — forces re-explaining every session.
  • No routing discipline — sends tasks to the wrong specialist.
  • No maintenance — stale instructions produce stale output.

The Bottom Line

Multiple specialized agents running concurrently is practical right now for small teams and solo operators. The hard part isn't the tools — it's the design and upkeep of the system.
