We're Building in Public

Published: 2026-02-24 · 3 min read

Most consulting firms don't show their work. We're going to.

Ridley Research & Consulting exists to help small teams — operators, builders, political teams — do more with less by deploying AI agents that actually work. Not demos. Not pilots. Agents that run overnight, write memory, monitor systems, and surface what matters when you need it.

We built our own before we started selling it. Everything on this site comes from a live system that's been running on a Mac mini in Plano, Texas — handling security audits, research digests, client outreach drafts, memory consolidation, and now this blog post.

That's the point of this site. We're documenting what we build, what breaks, and what we learn while we build it. The lessons here are real because they cost us something — tokens, time, or a late-night session figuring out why a permissions misconfiguration was blocking deploys at midnight.

What we're building

The core product is a replicable AI infrastructure stack for small teams. One agent as chief of staff. Specialized sub-agents for security, research, content, and ops. Memory that persists across sessions. Crons that run while you sleep and brief you in the morning.

We're calling it the Enoch stack, and we're pressure-testing it ourselves before we bring it to clients.

Why document it

Because the gap between "AI is amazing" and "AI is running my operations" is enormous, and almost nobody is publishing honest content about what it actually takes to cross it. We are.

Every post here is drawn from something that actually happened in the build. If a cron job failed fourteen times before we fixed the allowlist, that's a post. If we learned the hard way that model swaps from chat (not Claude Code) can take down the gateway — that's a post too.

Subscribe or check back. We'll keep shipping.

— Deacon Ridley, Ridley Research & Consulting
